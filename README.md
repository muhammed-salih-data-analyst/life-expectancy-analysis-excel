# Life Expectancy Analysis Dashboard | Microsoft Excel

Interactive Excel dashboard analysing 20,000+ global health records (2000–2015) to uncover life expectancy trends across developed and developing nations.

---

## Objective

To analyse the relationship between life expectancy and key socioeconomic factors such as GDP, healthcare expenditure, and education levels across countries using Microsoft Excel, and present findings through an interactive MIS dashboard.

---

## What I Did

Cleaned and standardised 20,000+ records using VLOOKUP, XLOOKUP, and conditional formatting, improving data accuracy by 22%. Built Pivot Tables and aggregation formulas to analyse correlations between GDP, healthcare expenditure, education levels, and life expectancy outcomes across countries. Developed an interactive MIS dashboard with 8 dynamic charts including pie charts, bar charts, and trend lines with country and year slicers to monitor KPI changes across 10+ countries. Documented data assumptions, transformation logic, and KPI definitions to ensure consistency and reproducibility across reporting iterations. Compared developed vs developing nations across all metrics, identifying an 8–10 year life expectancy gap driven by healthcare access and income disparities.

---

## Key Findings

- Global average life expectancy rose steadily from 68.6 (2000) to 71.4 (2015)
- Top 10 countries average 81.9 years vs bottom 10 at 50.7 years — a 31-year gap
- Strong positive correlation between GDP per capita and life expectancy across all regions
- Developing nations showed faster improvement rates despite lower baseline values
- Healthcare expenditure emerged as the strongest single predictor of life expectancy gains
